Rediscovering Lost Mid-Century Masterpieces

Welcome to the Visual Metaphysics Digital Recreation Project, dedicated to preserving and studying the innovative information charts created by Fred Keziah in the 1950s. These remarkable works combine philosophical exploration with the distinctive aesthetic sensibilities of mid-century modern design.

Each project in this collection represents a meticulous digital recreation based on archival research, bringing these lost treasures back into public view after decades of obscurity.

No. 1: Flying Saucers (1956)

A graphic colored chart picturing 228 drawings of saucer shapes, formations, maneuvers and spacepeople. Numerous listings of colors, sounds, speeds, sizes, etc. Originally published on July 2, 1956. Our recreation is based on the 2nd edition from 1958.

About the Visual Metaphysics Series

Fred S. Keziah (1920-2010) was a Navy-trained illustrator and animator who specialized in educational graphics and informational art. After his military service, he self-published a series of "information sheets" from his home in Arlington, Virginia.

The Visual Metaphysics series represents Keziah's ambitious attempt to encapsulate complex phenomena in single-page visual charts. Drawing on both scientific concepts and metaphysical traditions, these works occupy a unique intersection of mid-century design, information visualization, and spiritual exploration.

The series reflects the cultural tensions of the 1950s—a decade characterized by both rigid conformity and burgeoning counterculture movements. Keziah's work bridges these worlds, employing the precision and optimism of modernist design to explore unconventional subjects like UFOs and reincarnation that challenged mainstream scientific narratives.
